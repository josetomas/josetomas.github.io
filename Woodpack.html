SUSTAINABLE BY DESIGN

The New Materials Coop is a startup funded in the year 2019 by Paloma Gonzalez, Jose Antonio Gonzalez and myself. This company as part of our research with BioPlastics with the goal of advancing Woodpack into industrial scale production. Currently we are participating in the semifinals of MIT incubator DesignX with a pitch presentation on December 5.

WoodPack, our main product. It is a new biodegradable packaging film material from wood pulp that is high performance and scalable. The material achieves mechanical properties equivalent to those of LDPE/HDPE, is water resistant, and biodegrades in about eight weeks in a landfill. We are proud to have Arauco as an ally and as a wood pulp provider. The WoodPack formula was developed at MIT and is patent pending. Our goal is to industrially produce WoodPack, completely replacing LDPE/HDPE film.

Our product has gone through lab validation using infraestructure available in Center of Bits and Atoms, and it shows a mechanical strength of ~9.8 MPa, wich is comparable to plastic film (Polyethelene ~11 MPa, HDPE (film) ~7.6 MPa). It is also waterproof and biodegrades in 2 month.

We are creating a biodegradable plastic material to mass produce and easily dispose of without harming our planet. Woodpack is challenging the whole plastic supply chain and its "shipment to landfill" life cycle. It meets all the industrial protective packaging film requirements, high strength and optimal outdoor performance, adding landfill disposability.

Woodpack, our biodegradable packaging film, protects items during shipment, handling, and storage, with full reliance. It is manufactured from abundant materials and with advanced manufacturing techniques. It profits from inactive industrial materials and facilities. Woodpack is cheap, handy and its raw materials are massively available around the world. The product aims to not only serve as an environmentally conscious alternative, but also serve as a composition enhanced formula, optimized for protection in the shipping and safeguarding of our clients products. This project was born out of biomaterial and fabrication research conducted by graduate students at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. The combined knowledge in material science, advanced manufacturing, product development and experience in the construction market created The New Materials Co-op Company, with Woodpack as our flag product.
