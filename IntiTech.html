AUTONOMOUS DEVICE FOR PV PANEL CLEANING

Inti-Tech developed an autonomous robot fleet for solar panel mainteinance, they are intended to clean the panels reducing the ineficiency due to soiling. The robots don't use water, are modular, doesn't need to modify the solar panels, and adpats to any posible array.

During my period working in the company we went from a "proof of concept" (image in the left) to a comercial product (image in the right), currently this version is being mass produced an installed in diferent solar farms in Chile.

I worked in the development of different expriments:

DICTUC: We worked with the certification institute of Pontificia Universidad Catolica de Chile, where we looked to certificate that the robots produced and increase in the solar panels eficiency.

Laborelec: The research institution from the electric generator company Engie. We conducted an on site experiment in the solar farm "Pampa Camarones", located near the city of Arica, Chile in the middle of the Atacama Dessert, a extremenly tough enviorment; it is the driest desert in the world, has extremly high solar radiation levels, and thick soil that damages mechanical systems. This characteristics have make this enviorment comparable to Mars [1][2]

The objective of this experiment were:

  • Test how robots will behave in such an extreme enviorment.
  • An accelerated aging test, that simulated 10 years of use in 6 month.
  • Test if the cleaning system installed in the robots produced "micro cracks" in the solar panels.
  • Test if a real increase in the solar panel array eficiency was produced by this cleaning method.
