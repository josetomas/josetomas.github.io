José Tomás Domínguez

Mechanical engineer and designer. I have dedicated the last 3 years of my life to understand and develop new materials and advanced manufacturing processes for sustainable new materials. Pushing the world toward a circular economy.

This process started in 2017. I was working as part of the R&D team of one of the first generation of Chilean robotic startups, Inti Tech. We were developing a solar autonomous robot that cleaned solar panels. I was passionate about changing the world through my work, contributing to the clean energy revolution. During my on-site expedition to Pampa Camarones, a solar farm from Engie, I reflected on the pollution generated in the solar panel’s supply chain. It simply didn’t make sense to me that these new technologies had such a negative impact on the environment. This lead me to understand that my role in this changing world wasn’t in energy but in the development of biodegradable new materials that changed the way we built our society.

That make me resign from that job, in 2019 and take a scholarship from the Chilean government to travel to Boston and research solutions for this problem. I was working at Dassault Systems 3D experience Lab when I met Paloma Gonzalez, an MIT Ph.D. researching new natural fiber-based biodegradable materials. Together we started working on the development of new compostable materials made up of industrial waste. Three years from that I have developed 2 patentable projects (porous structure design method for filtration of natural fibers and a novel wood cellulose bioplastic film), and a startup dedicated to our research, The New Materials, which had lead me to collaborate with the industry biggest companies/organizations (e.g. Arauco, CMPC, HP, Formlabs, IMFA, among others).
