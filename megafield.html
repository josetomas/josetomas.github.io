INTERACTIVE USER INTERFACE FOR ROBOTIC EDUCATION

MegaField is an interactive education environment that empowers schools’ kids to learn robotics. To make this possible MegaBrain is developing a digitally sensorized robotic table that interacts with kids through sounds and lights every time they accomplish a specific task.

MegaField goes beyond the GUI + Lego MindStorm classic robotic class. It integrates a responsive environment through a physical device that interacts with the robots and the user measuring and reacting to the accomplishment of challenges designed by the teachers. It is inspired by the concept of Tangible User Interface defined by Hiroshi Ishii.

The table has adaptable visual environments specially designed for school curriculum and to stimulate learning. It delivers scores as the challenges and game tasks are overcome, fostering the competition to learn inherited from educational robotics.

My contribution for this project was the development of a proof of concept. I developed the first table, integrating sounds and lights and design key features. I also contributed to the design of the challenges. Working in the whole kids experience and studying its interaction with the device. The prime objective of this first version was to study the reaction of kids to different stimuli. Also test the different “missions” designed by the professors and evaluate the level of interest from the students to create a library of missions.

Currently the project has been funded as part of the “Technology Transfer” plan of Universidad Tecnica Federico Santa Maria, and they are developing a finished product to test kids reactions and stimulis preferences. Chileans universities are closed, so the project is frozen.
