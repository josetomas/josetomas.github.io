DIGITAL DATA ADQUISITION FOR KINESIOLOGY

Khapto aims to revolutionise the physiotherapy industry. This through the development of a wearable device that measures key dynamic parameters of the patients motion such as force and displacement. All these parameters are currently read by analog old fashioned devices.

We developed a novel device that complimented the physiotherapist perception of the patients health with data. A key in the physiotherapist work is his own haptic perception, our device complimented this through a sensorized wearable glove. The goal was to augment the information specialist could get from touching their patients.

My contribution for this start-up was centered in the process of building a robust product that could be validated in a real work environment by technical labs. During the period that I worked in this company we advanced from a mock-up version of the product to a product ready for validation. My tasks were; component selection, sensor calibration, hardware and firmware development and anti bacterial material testing.

Further, here I explain in more detail what my contribution was in two specific experiments

  1. Kinesic Work: A physical rehabilitation center in Santiago, Chile. The company conducted a client validation experiment where they had the chance to test our product directly with their clients, in their therapy process. This was part of a market validation grant the company won from the chilean state agency CORFO.

    My contribution: to this experiment changed within the scope of it. Before the experiment started I worked in the development of the new version of the wearable device that was going to be applied in the experiment. During the pilot I was in charge of documenting the users issues with the product, and iterating new versions.

This experiment was my first approach to human computer interaction, through hardware. My task was to make a pull of information from the user, physiotherapist, to build a device that suited their needs. One of the biggest challenges was to upfront the users rejection to learn new tools, especially when they are in a prototype stage.

Final Product

Before leaving the company I tooked part of the team that developed the roadmap for a final, massive production product. I worked specifically in the electronics and firmware.
